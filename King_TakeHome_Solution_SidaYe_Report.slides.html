Conclusion: So, according to the test on general players conversion rate from group B is statistically significant than that from group A. While when we take a look at new players and old players separately, we can see there is no statistically significant for each segment. This is a simpson's paradox which is a trend appears in several different groups of data but disappears or reverses when these groups are combined.

Part 4: Recommendation and Conclusion¶

I would like NOT to implement this change. I list a few reasons below.

  • I think we need to adjust the distribution of new and old players in our experiment period. Otherwise, the test result has bias. So, when I analyzed the result, I looked at three views(general, new player and old player) It turns out that within the treatment group, both new and old players played less game rounds. While for conversion rate, the overall segment indicates that there is a statiscial significant. However, when I looked at new and old players separately, there is no statistical significant. It is a simpson's paradox.
  • Both new players and old players has less engagement towards the treatment. While, new players are more likely to have a conversion then old players under the treatment.
  • I think the treatment is trying to increase the difficulty level of the game. For old players, they would like to play more rounds in order to pass it. While for new players, they found it is difficult. So, they prefer to spend some money to purchase either lifes or supporting items to pass it.
  • Even for short-terms, we have a high conversion for new users. It will hurt our old players' experience. And old players are the majority of our game users. We need to let them have a positive gameing experience.
